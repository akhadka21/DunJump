# BASIC
Player avatar movement. The player must be able to move horizontally, and must be capable of at least single jumping. 
✅

Ground and platforms. The game level must have at least one ground and one platform type of tile and associated behavior.
Collectible items. The game level must have at least one kind of collectible item. 
✅

End of level condition. There must be some way for the player to complete a level. 
✅ Kill all enemies, collect all coins and pass through the portal

Level size. The level must be roughly three screen widths wide (that is, a fair bit wider than it is tall, hence requiring some amount of camera scrolling to access the initially hidden elements of the level). So long as the level involves horizontal scrolling to access initially hidden elements, you will receive full credit here. 
✅

Scrolling & camera. The game must scroll the gameworld as the player moves through it, and must have a designed camera behavior. 
✅

Particle Juice. The game must use a particle system to communicate horizontal player avatar movement, and another particle system to communicate vertical (jumping) player avatar movement. The horizontal movement particle system needs to look different from the system provided in S7: Platformers ❤️ ✨ + 📷, such as by changing the number of particles emitted (fewer would be better), changing the texture used (pick another set from the tile sheet provided), changing the emission rate, and others. Look at other platformer games for ideas on how to make these particles look better (the movement particle system used in the section assignment could definitely look better!). 
✅

Audio juice. The game must use audio effects to help communicate at least one of the following actions: jumping, moving, landing, or collecting the collectibles. 
✅

# BONUS

Parallax background layers (1 point). Add some depth to your world by having scrolling parallax backgrounds. 
✅

Complex camera (up to 2 points). Implement a camera deadzone (0.5 points), and have the deadzone adapt to the player's movement (another 0.5 points). Include complex behavior such as vertically rising to the new platformer level (may require a level which is tall to allow for vertical movement)(up to 1 point) 
✅

Double jumping and wall jumping (1 point). This also implies a level design which has been created with double/wall jumping in mind. 
✅ Powerup that allows for a single use of double jump

Ways for player to die/be incapacitated (up to 1 point, depending on complexity). Whether it's water, lava, spikes, spears, saw blades, pits or bullets, it's just so easy for the player to die or be temporarily incapacitated in platformer worlds. These elements can be passive (lava, water, pits) or active (a spear shoots out if the player moves over a trigger location). If your player avatar can be harmed, then your game also needs a player health/lives mechanic, 
✅ Can be killed by enemies

Enemies (up to 1 point). Platformer games often have some kind of enemy which either impedes player movement or harms the player. Enemies typically move along a fixed path. 0.5 points for any enemy at all, an additional 0.5 points for movement and an animated movement cycle. 
✅ Enemies move a random amount is any direction and have an basic animation and fire at the player IF they are in proximity

Power-ups (0.5 points). An item which grants the player modified or enhanced capabilities. This can include extra health, ability to avoid damage (shield), greater speed, larger/smaller size, ability to ride on top of another creature, and more.
✅ Double jump powerup and heal

Dynamic level elements (up to 2 points based on complexity). The player can cause aspects of the level to appear/disappear/change. For example, a platform could appear or disappear based on the player reaching some trigger location, or a climbable vine could appear after the player destroys a destructible platformer tile, etc. Simple appearing/disappearing is 1 point. Dynamic modification based on level progression, etc. gets you into the 1-2 point range. 
▶️ Basic wall that falls when all enemies are defeated and all coins are collected allowing the player to progress to the next level

Interactive items (up to 2 points, depending on complexity). Items in the game world the player can interact with. These can include simple elements such as signs or levers, or very complex elements such as conversations with other player characters, or full in-game item stores.
▶️ Simple items like coins, powerups, heals and springs

Animated level elements (1 point). Inclusion of at least two different types of animated tiles in the level, along with animated collectibles.
▶️ All interactive items have animations and the background has moving fans
